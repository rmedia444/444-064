The Emergence of Crowdfunding

Crowdfunding platforms like Kickstarter and Indiegogo were emerging in 2010, offering new ways for entrepreneurs, artists, and creators to fund their projects. These platforms allowed individuals to raise small amounts of money from a large number of people, democratizing access to capital and enabling innovative ideas to come to fruition.

The success of crowdfunding campaigns demonstrated the power of community support and the potential of this funding model to disrupt traditional financing methods. The emergence of crowdfunding provided a lifeline for creative projects and startups, fostering a culture of innovation and collaboration.
